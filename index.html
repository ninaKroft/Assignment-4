Aerospace engineering


What is aerospace engineering?

Aerospace engineers design and develop anything that flies inside or outside of the atmosphere. Two seperate branches were created to divide aircraft and spacecraft. These branches are: astronautical engineering - which deals with everything that flies outside of the atmosphere - and aeronautical engineering - which deals with everything that flies within the atmosphere. Because of the size and complexity of these projects, they are usually worked on by a team of engineers.


Education required

To become an aerospace engineer, you are required to go to university and to have at least a bachelor's degree, which can take 4 years to complete. You can expect to spend 2 years to earn a master's degree, then an additional 3 years to earn a Ph.D. Some universities offer courses specific to astronautical and aeronautical engineering, but most universities offer an aerospace engineering course which covers both branches.
