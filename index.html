Aerospace engineering


What is aerospace engineering?

Aerospace engineers design and develop anything that flies inside or outside
of the atmosphere, as well as the programs that controlls the systems. Two seperate
branches were created to divide aircraft and spacecraft. These branches are: astronautical
engineering - which deals with everything that flies outside of the atmosphere - and
aeronautical engineering - which deals with everything that flies within the atmosphere.
Because of the size and complexity of these projects, they are usually worked on by a team of engineers.

Education required

To become an aerospace engineer, you are required to go to university and to
have at least a bachelor's degree, which can take 4 years to complete. You can expect
to spend 2 years to earn a master's degree, then an additional 3 years to earn a Ph.D.
Some universities offer courses specific to astronautical and aeronautical engineering, but
most universities offer an aerospace engineering course which covers both branches.

Job info


Job outlook for aerospace engineering

There are many future job opportunities in the future for aerospace engineers,
because it is a growing field of work that is very unlikely to be replaced by computers.
The employment of aerospace engineers is expected to grow by 6% from 2021 to 2031, which is
as fast as average. There are predicted to be 3,800 openings for this field every year on
average over the next ten years.


Salary range

The average yearly salary for an aerospace engineer in Canada is $108,625, which
is around $55.67 per hour. Entry level salaries are around $87,263 per year, while the
highest earners earn around $157,663 per year.

Education at Carleton University


Entrance requirements

To be eligible to apply for the aerospace engineering course at Carleton University,
you need to take a minimum of six 4U/M courses. Four of these courses must be: advanced
functions, chemistry, physics, and one of the following: calculus and vectors, biology, or
earth and space science. Also, you must have your Ontario high school diploma (OSSD) or equivalent.

How would a career in aerospace engineering have a positive impact on the people around me?

The technology that aerospace engineers design and maintain play
a large role in human society. By improving aircraft so that they are faster,
safer, and more fuel efficient, aerospace engineers are making people's quality
of life and security in the air better. Also, the spacecraft that they build not
only provide a sense of wonder, but also provide valuable data that help give us insight
on our world. As example, there are weather satellites that are collecting valuable
data on the composition of the Earth's atmosphere, which helps climate activists
and scientists come up with solutions to global warming while keeping big contributors accountable.
